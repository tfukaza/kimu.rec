The Robert Welch Kingham Dinner cutlery is the top pick because the shape is simple, balanced, and easy to live with. 

Some light utensils get there by using long, thin tines that feel nimble but can become too delicate. The opposite problem is a short-tined ones with too much rounded metal behind the tines, which starts to feel somewhat awkward.

The MUJI Stainless Steel Straight Handle Fork is the counterexample to check against. It has the appealing MUJI basics package: slim flat handle, 18-8 stainless steel, matte hairline finish, dishwasher-safe, and a low price. But if the head shape reads as short-pronged or spork-like in hand, that is exactly the kind of minimalist tradeoff to avoid here.
