A dish rack is only worth dedicating counter space to if it handles drainage well. The common failure mode is not capacity, but the tray underneath getting gross because water sits there instead of moving back into the sink.

The simplehuman Steel Frame Dishrack is the practical pick because the drainage spout is built into the design and the major parts can be removed for cleaning. That matters more over time than the first-week appearance, because dish racks live in one of the wettest and highest-maintenance parts of the kitchen.
