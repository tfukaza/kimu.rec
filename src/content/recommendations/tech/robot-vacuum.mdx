Modern robot vacuums do more than just vacuum. 
They can self-charge, empty its own dust, mop hardwood, then self-clean the mop, avoid obstacles, so on.
When choosing the "best" robot vacuum, you want to look for one that beats its competitors in every aspect.

### Navigation & Obstacle Avoidance
Robot vacuums bumping into walls and dog poops are things of the past, but it's also far from a solved problem.
Always check independent reviews and online comments to judge which robot can navigate complex floor plans without crashing into things. 
You can also check the technology being used to make an educated guess about its capabilities.
<ul className="consideration-point-list">
  <li>Although rare these days, some models use camera-based _navigation_. Still seen in old or cheap models, it can struggle in cramped areas or complex floor plans.</li>
  <li>LiDAR is increasingly common for a wide range of robots. 
      Compared to cameras, it gives the robot a more detailed sense of the room layout.</li>
  <li>Cameras are still common for obstacle avoidance. 
      Paired with image recognition software, it allows robots to intelligenty recognize and avoid obstacles.</li>
  <li>Some models use front-mounted lasers to map out obstacles.</li>
  <li>High end models often combine camera and laser based obstacle avoidance.</li>
</ul>


### Dust Collection
More suction is not automatically better. Brush design and other mechanisms can matter just as much.
<ul className="consideration-point-list">
    <li>Pet owners should look closely at anti-tangle brush designs.</li>
    <li>Check for robots that have mechanisms to tackle edge and corner cleaning, like extending side brushes.</li>
</ul>


### Mopping
Mopping systems are one of the biggest differences between basic and flagship robot vacuums.
<ul className="consideration-point-list">
    <li>Vibrating pads are essentially cloth pads rubbed against the floor. They are common on older models and usually clean less effectively.</li>
    <li>Rotating pads are spinning cloth pads. They are more common now and generally clean better than vibrating pads.</li>
    <li>Roller pads use a spinning cylinder of cloth to scrub the floor while continuously collecting dirty water into a tank.</li>
</ul>

### Mop Lift and Detach
If your home has both carpet and hard floors, mop handling becomes important.
<ul className="consideration-point-list">
    <li>Some robots lift the mop when crossing rugs or carpets.</li>
    <li>Some models can fully detach and reattach the mop at the dock.</li>
    <li>Without this, you may need to manually remove the mop whenever you switch floor types.</li>
</ul>

### Dock
The dock is no longer just a charger. On high-end models, it can remove a lot of routine maintenance.
<ul className="consideration-point-list">
    <li>Auto-empty docks collect dust from the robot after each run, usually into a disposable bag or reusable bin.</li>
    <li>Auto-refill docks can refill the robot's clean-water tank and drain dirty water.</li>
    <li>Mop-cleaning docks may wash mop pads with heated water and dry them with heated air.</li>
</ul>

### Clearance
Shorter robots can fit under furniture more easily, but often lack some features.
<ul className="consideration-point-list">
<li>Top-mounted LiDAR can be a blocker for lot of tight spaces. Some brands try to embed the lidar into the rear of the robot, but this doesn't reduce 
    the height as much and can also diminish navigation performance. Others make top mounted LiDARs that can retract if needed.</li>
<li>Measure furniture clearance and compare it against the robot's listed dimensions.</li>
</ul>

### App
Software quality matters.
<ul className="consideration-point-list">
  <li>Look for features like no-go zones, multi-floor maps, room-by-room cleaning, and cleaning history.</li>
  <li>Check app-store reviews for complaints about lag, bugs, account issues, or security concerns.</li>
</ul>

### Maintenance
The best robot vacuums are easy to take apart and clean.
<ul className="consideration-point-list">
    <li>Check whether brushes, filters, trays, water tanks, and dock parts can be removed without tools.</li>
    <li>Cheaper models may hide maintenance problems behind parts that are difficult to disassemble.</li>
</ul>

## Verdict

The Dreame X60 Ultra is the top pick because it checks off all the bells and whistles you can every want from a vacuum:
- High suction, well designed brush, extending side brushes for corners.
- Dock to recharge, self empty dust, and refill water.
- Retracts the LiDAR if needed to fit under furniture.
- Advanced obstacle avoidance.
- Detachable mop pads to avoid soaking the carpet after mopping.
- Uses hot water for mopping, built in dryer in dock to dry mop after cleaning.

The only potential downside is that it a spinning mop instead of a roller mop, that's not necessarily a bad thing
unless your have _really_ messy hard floors that need heavy cleaning. In fact in most cases spinner mops are
superior since they are easier to maintain. Overall this vacuum brings the best package a vacuum can offer.
